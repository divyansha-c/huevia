Behind the Scenes

The Journey of a Painting

From a blank canvas and a feeling, to a finished piece of art — here's how every painting comes to life.

My Approach

Art as a process, not a product.

I never rush a painting. Each piece goes through several stages — sketching, layering, revising, and refining — until it feels complete. Sometimes that takes days; sometimes it takes weeks.

I believe the process is just as important as the finished painting. The layers you can't always see in the final work — the pencil sketch underneath, the blocked-in tones, the experiments — are all part of what makes a painting alive.

I share as much of this journey as I can on Instagram, and I love when collectors get to see how their painting was made.

The Stages

From idea to artwork

Stage 1

The Sketch

Every painting begins with a pencil sketch. I loosely map out the composition on canvas before any paint touches the surface.

Stage 2

Blocking In

I block in the main shapes and tones with broad, confident strokes. This is the most exciting stage — the painting comes alive quickly.

Stage 3

Building Layers

Layer by layer, I build up depth, texture, and detail. For oils, this can take multiple sessions with drying time in between.

Stage 4

Details & Texture

The finishing stage — adding small details, highlights, texture strokes, and that final touch that makes the painting feel complete.

Stage 5

Varnishing & Finishing

I varnish every completed oil and acrylic painting to protect it and give it a beautiful, even finish — matte or glossy depending on the piece.

Stage 6

Packing with Care

Each painting is wrapped in glassine paper, padded, boxed, and sent with a handwritten note and a certificate of authenticity.
